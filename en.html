Research Interests

Digital governance is where my scholarship began, and the ground from which I approach AI governance.

  • My work centers on smart government, extending from government data governance to AI algorithmic governance—both ultimately directed at the realization of public value.
  • I build research and policy tools hands-on with generative AI, integrating them into academic research workflows so that analytical capacity becomes a working application.
  • I specialize in quantitative methods centered on structural equation modeling (SEM), testing the antecedents and mechanisms behind governance behavior in the public sector.
  • More than a decade in the civil service, together with teaching experience in public law, lets me read from the front line how regulation is translated and enforced—and bring scholarship and practice into dialogue.

My current research focuses on technology-enabled law enforcement, organized around the idea of the gatekeeper under the algorithm: decision bias, disruption of professional judgment, and the construction of legitimacy in AI-assisted enforcement.
